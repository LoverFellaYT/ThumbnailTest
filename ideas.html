Title Review Rubric

Category 1 – Needs Work 2 – Solid 3 – Excellent 4 – Exceptional
Clarity & Readability Confusing or unclear wording, too long, or overloaded with jargon. Hard for viewers to grasp the video topic quickly. Understandable in parts, but somewhat vague or wordy. Requires effort to see what the video is about. Mostly clear, concise, and easy to comprehend. The main idea is apparent without much effort. Extremely clear and straightforward. Any viewer can instantly tell what the video covers. No unnecessary wording or confusion.
Clickability & CTR Unappealing or generic. Doesn’t motivate viewers to click. Likely overlooked in feeds or search results. Mildly interesting but lacks a strong hook or emotional pull. Some viewers may click, but not enough for broad appeal. Fairly engaging. Offers a decent hook or value proposition. Likely to achieve an average or slightly above-average CTR. Highly compelling and attention-grabbing. Strong emotional or value-based hook encourages a wide range of viewers to click immediately.
Curiosity & Intrigue Lacks mystery or excitement. Gives away too much or is too vague to spark curiosity. Somewhat intriguing, but either reveals too much or doesn’t fully draw viewers in. Creates a moderate curiosity gap, withholding just enough to make viewers want to learn more. Masterfully arouses curiosity. Teases something unique or surprising without spoiling, prompting viewers to click and find out the details.
Power Words & Emotion Flat, neutral language with no memorable phrases. Minimal emotional resonance. Contains a few engaging words but not strongly emotive. Some appeal, but not maximized. Uses strong verbs and adjectives, eliciting a noticeable emotional or interest-based response. Language is highly emotive and riveting, using well-chosen words (e.g., “insane,” “ultimate,” “unbelievable”) that strongly grab attention.
Length & Structure Too long/short or poorly structured. Key info gets truncated or lost. Viewer struggles to see the core message. Acceptable length but not fully optimized. May risk truncation on some devices or bury the main point too far in. Within an ideal range (around 60 characters or fewer). Main idea is visible and not cut off. Perfectly concise and well-structured. No risk of truncation, and the hook is front-loaded for maximum impact.
Numbers & Novelty No mention of interesting numbers, stakes, or fresh twists. Feels generic or routine. Includes some numeric or novelty element, but not especially powerful or original. Showcases a clear number or unique aspect that adds interest (e.g., time challenges, top lists, big goals). Powerfully highlights large or unusual numbers, extreme stakes, or an unseen twist, immediately signaling uniqueness and excitement.
Accuracy & Honesty Misleading or clickbait. Promises something that isn’t delivered, causing viewer disappointment. Partially exaggerated or overhyped. The video somewhat matches the title, but viewers may feel let down. Mostly accurate and honest, with mild exaggeration at most. Viewers generally get what the title promises. Completely true to the content. The title is exciting yet fully accurate, meeting or exceeding viewer expectations once they watch.
Formatting & Word Choice Disorganized wording. May start with unimportant info or repeat unnecessary phrases. Adequate structure but not polished. The main idea might be placed late or duplicated words could be trimmed. Well-structured and concise. Hook or key terms appear early. Little to no redundancy. Exceptionally formatted and concise. Important details appear at the start. Every word serves a purpose, with no fluff or repetition.
